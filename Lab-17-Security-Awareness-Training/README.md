# Lab 17 – Security Awareness Training

## Overview

This lab demonstrates how to design and deliver a basic cybersecurity awareness training session. The focus is on educating users about common cyber threats such as phishing, password attacks, and social engineering. Participants also complete a short quiz and provide feedback to identify knowledge gaps for future awareness programs.

---

## Objectives

- Understand the importance of security awareness.
- Learn how phishing attacks work.
- Understand password security best practices.
- Recognize social engineering techniques.
- Create a cybersecurity awareness presentation.
- Conduct a security awareness quiz.
- Analyze participant feedback.

## Task 1 – Create a Security Awareness Presentation

A presentation was created covering one of the most common cybersecurity threats: **Phishing**.

Presentation structure included:

- Introduction to Phishing
- How Phishing Works
- Types of Phishing Attacks
- Real-World Examples
- Indicators of a Phishing Email
- Best Practices
- Reporting Suspicious Emails

Visual diagrams and screenshots were included to improve understanding.

---

## Task 2 – Conduct a Security Awareness Quiz

A short quiz was prepared to evaluate participant understanding.

Sample questions included:

- What is phishing?
- How do you identify a fake email?
- Should you click unknown links?
- What makes a strong password?
- What should you do if you suspect phishing?

The quiz was created using Google Forms and shared with participants.

---

## Task 3 – Collect Feedback

Participants provided feedback after completing the training.

Feedback focused on:

- Training clarity
- Ease of understanding
- Confidence in identifying phishing attacks
- Suggestions for improvement

Knowledge gaps identified during the feedback process will be addressed in future awareness sessions.
